Proposal for Creativity Competition

Project Title

Comprehensive Learning App — Smart Educational Solution for Oromia, Ethiopia, and Beyond

Prepared by: Eden Daba Kelbesa

1. Introduction

Education is a vital pathway to a better future. However, many students in Oromia, Ethiopia, and across Africa face significant challenges such as lack of quality resources, limited personalized support, and unreliable internet access. These challenges hinder academic success and limit future opportunities, especially for students in underserved communities and boarding schools.

The Comprehensive Learning App is an innovative, offline-capable educational tool designed to empower students by transforming how they study and interact with learning materials. Developed with the realities of boarding school students in mind, this app combines smart technology and creative features to address core educational challenges while promoting equity, accessibility, and sustainability. It offers a holistic solution that meets the diverse needs of students from different backgrounds and abilities, with a strong focus on inclusiveness and practicality.

2. Problem Statement with Data

Students in Oromia and across Ethiopia face several educational obstacles that affect their progress and motivation:

  • Limited Access to Clear Study Notes: Many students do not have access to concise, well-structured notes aligned with their classroom lessons.
  • Lack of Practice Tools: Students rarely have personalized question banks or instant exam preparation tools.
  • Unreliable Internet Access: Only 21.3% of Ethiopians have reliable internet access, leaving about 78.7% without adequate connectivity (Ethiopian Communications Authority, 2024).
  • Online Distractions: Over 65% of students are distracted by entertainment content on their phones during study (UNESCO, 2023).
  • Dependence on Paper Books: Carrying multiple bulky textbooks burdens families financially and contributes to deforestation, with Ethiopia losing 1.5 million hectares of forest annually due to paper demand (FAO, 2023).
  • Hearing Disabilities: More than 30% of students in special education have hearing impairments and lack suitable tools (Ministry of Education Report, 2023).

These challenges restrict student achievement and widen educational inequality.

3. Project Objectives

  • Bilingual Chatbot (Afaan Oromoo & English): Provides instant answers to student questions.
  • Smart Quiz Generator: Creates personalized quizzes from typed or spoken notes.
  • Summary Note Feature: Condenses long topics into digestible summaries.
  • Offline Notepad: Allows note-taking without internet.
  • Motivational Videos & Educational Games: Enhances student engagement and motivation.
  • 100% Offline Functionality: Eliminates dependency on internet and reduces distractions.
  • Digital Alternative to Books: Cuts costs and environmental damage from printing.
  • Audio-to-Text Feature: Transcribes spoken words to text to assist hearing-impaired students.
  • Word and Sentence Translator (English ↔ Amharic ↔ Afaan Oromoo): Supports multilingual understanding.

4. Creative Story and Inspiration

Before boarding school, I struggled without quality notes or help when stuck. Many peers shared these difficulties, some dropping out due to language barriers and lack of resources.

Learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, I developed a simple offline app to write notes and generate questions. Inspired by a hearing-impaired friend, I added audio-to-text transcription and a translator for Amharic, Afaan Oromoo, and English. Now, the app helps students study independently and clearly — all offline.

5. Process and Tools Used

  • Developed with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for responsive design.
  • Planned integration of offline speech-to-text models.
  • Built a simple NLP chatbot for Afaan Oromoo and English.
  • Created language translation between Amharic, Afaan Oromoo, and English.
  • Designed quiz logic to generate questions from notes.
  • Embedded motivational videos and memory games.
  • Tested on low-end Android devices to ensure smooth offline use.

6. Expanded Impact

  • Students: Study anytime, anywhere, without internet or bulky books. Practice with smart quizzes and translations.
  • Disabled Learners: Access transcribed content and learn at their pace.
  • Environment: Reduce printing and deforestation by digitalizing study materials.
  • Beyond Ethiopia: Adaptable for other African countries and languages.

  • Phase 1: Finalize UI and offline features.
  • Phase 2: Pilot test at current boarding school, gather feedback.

  • Phase 4: Rollout to Oromia schools, then nationally and regionally.

9. Conclusion and Empowerment Message

The Comprehensive Learning App is a transformative tool addressing the real struggles of rural and under-resourced students. It supports smart, offline learning; hearing disability inclusion; distraction reduction; and environmental sustainability.

This app demonstrates how one student’s creativity and determination can ignite widespread change.

To all students: Your challenges do not limit your potential. With persistence, innovation, and support, you can overcome barriers and build your future. This app is a tool—but you are the true agents of change.
